The word "much" is a small but powerful word that helps us talk about how big, important, or plentiful something is. For example, we might say, "I don't have much money," to describe a small amount of money, or "I had so much fun at the party," to describe a lot of fun.

We can also use "much" in questions, like "How much time do we have left?" or "How much does this cost?" In these cases, "much" helps us ask about the amount of something we're interested in.

Overall, "much" is a handy word that helps us describe and ask about quantities, and it's an important part of everyday English.

much: 多（的），大量（的）
