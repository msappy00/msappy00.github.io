Soft Drink v. Soda:
Soft drinks are any beverages that contain no alcohol. Coca-Cola, for example, doesn’t contain alcohol.
But it’s also soda. The drinks get their names because they’re made with carbonated water and the name comes from the sodium salts in the water. That’s where the phrase soda water comes from. Sodas, on the other hand, are more than just water. Typically, they’re made up of carbonated water, sweeteners, and natural or artificial flavors.
So, are all soft drinks sodas? Technically, no. A soft drink is just a drink without alcohol, and it doesn’t have to be carbonated. However, if you ask for a list of soft drinks, you’ll likely get a list of sodas. And all sodas aren’t soft drinks, either. You can buy hard sodas.
If you’ve ever wondered if soda and soft drinks are different, technically, they are. But let’s be honest, if you ask for one or the other, you’ll get the same thing.
